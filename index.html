Early life

Charles Barkley was born on February 20, 1963, in Leeds, Alabama. He was raised primarily by his mother, Charcey Glenn. During highschool, he didn't receive any offers from any major Division I programs.

Collegiate Career

Barkley played Center for the Auburn Tigers, from 1981-1984. He averaged over 20 points per game and was later named the SEC's Player of the Decade for the 1980s. His #34 jersey was retired from Auburn in the early 2000s.

NBA Career

Charles was selected 5th overall in the NBA Draft, by the 76ers. During his 16 year career, he also went on to play for the Rockets and Suns. While he never won an NBA Championship, he's still remembered as one of the greats.

Life after Retirement

Since retiring from the NBA, Charles has co-hosted NBA on TNT, alongside Kenny "The Jet" Smith, Shaquille O'Neal, and Ernie Johnson. His candidness makes him a great addition to the crew.
